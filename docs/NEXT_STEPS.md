# Next Steps

## Immediate Priorities

### 1. Finish Google Calendar confidence

- validate create, update, and delete flows end to end
- keep sync feedback explicit and calm
- keep time zone handling predictable
- remove any remaining "did it actually sync?" ambiguity

### 2. Strengthen care detail seriousness

- make medication, vaccine, and vet record detail feel complete
- keep historical vs active care state easy to read
- keep next steps legible without clutter

### 3. Keep health document retrieval sharp

- keep document editing/linking calm on mobile
- make key paperwork fast to open and verify
- preserve the trust value of searchable, linked documents

### 4. Refine PawPal carefully

- keep PawPal anchored as a care coordinator
- improve precision of follow-through suggestions
- make sure actions open the exact next useful step
- keep it useful, not chatty

### 5. Continue codebase cleanup where it reduces risk

- keep decomposing `App.tsx`
- preserve the trust layer as a clear subsystem
- add tests around high-risk product flows, not vanity coverage

## Secondary Priorities

### 6. Keep cross-device confidence strong

- continue validating backup and restore flows on realistic second-device/browser paths
- verify photo and health document recovery regularly
- keep restore outcomes unmistakable

### 7. Improve overall mobile polish

- keep reducing clutter in dense Care surfaces
- tighten form layout and action placement
- keep the app feeling calm on smaller screens

## Parked For Later

- basic phone back/native-feel controls
- richer routing or deep-link behavior

These are good future UX improvements, but they are not the current highest-leverage work.

## Explicitly Deferred

- email reminders
- multi-pet
- shared caregivers
- LLM-backed PawPal
- native mobile rewrite

These remain real future options, but they are not the best use of attention right now.
